Summary

The whole plot of Ulysses takes place on a single day (June 16, 1904) in and around Dublin. The novel's three central characters—Stephen Dedalus (the hero of Joyce's earlier Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man); Leopold Bloom, a Jewish advertising canvasser; and his wife, Molly—are meant to be modern counterparts of Telemachus, Ulysses (Odysseus), and Penelope, and the events of the novel loosely parallel Odysseus' journey home after the Trojan War.

James Joyce

James Augustine Aloysius Joyce was an Irish author and poet who lived from 2 February 1882 to 13 January 1941. He was a member of the modernist avant-garde and is considered as one of the twentieth century's most influential and important authors. Joyce is best known for Ulysses (1922), a seminal work in which Homer's Odyssey scenes are replicated in a variety of different literary styles, probably most notably the stream-of-consciousness approach he employed. The short story collection Dubliners (1914), as well as the novels A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man (1916) and Finnegans Wake (1939), are among well-known works by Joyce. His other works include three poetry collections, a play, sporadic journalism, and published correspondence.
